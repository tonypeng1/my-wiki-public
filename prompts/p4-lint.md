Perform a full health check on the wiki. Work through these 
checks in order:

1. Read wiki/index.md and all files in wiki/concepts/.

2. MISSING CONCEPT PAGES
   Identify every [[backlink]] referenced across all concept 
   articles that does not have a corresponding file in 
   wiki/concepts/. List them all.
   For each missing concept, create a stub article in 
   wiki/concepts/ with an Overview section and a note 
   marking it as a stub needing expansion.

3. THIN ARTICLES
   Identify concept articles that have fewer than 150 words 
   of substantive content (excluding frontmatter). List them.
   For each thin article, check if any summary files or 
   other concept articles contain relevant information that 
   could expand it. If yes, expand the article by integrating 
   that information.

4. CONTRADICTIONS
   Look for claims across different concept articles that 
   directly contradict each other. List any you find with 
   the specific conflicting statements and the files they 
   appear in. Do not resolve them automatically — flag 
   them for my review.

5. MISSING BACKLINKS
   Look for concept articles that clearly relate to each 
   other but do not yet link to each other. Add the missing 
   backlinks where appropriate.

6. NEW ARTICLE CANDIDATES
   Based on your reading of the full wiki, identify 3-5 
   new concept articles that would meaningfully improve 
   the wiki's coverage. For each, explain why it would 
   be valuable and which existing articles would link to it.

7. Write a health check report to wiki/maintenance/health-check-{today's date}.md
   with sections: wiki state summary · missing concepts created · thin articles expanded
   · contradictions flagged · backlinks added · recommended new articles.

8. Update wiki/index.md with an entry for the health check report.
